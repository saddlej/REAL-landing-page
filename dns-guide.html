Website Verification Guide

How to verify your domain

This takes about 2 minutes. No technical knowledge needed — just follow the steps below.

What is a TXT record? Think of it like a notice you pin to your domain's front door. It doesn't change your website or affect anything visible — it just lets REAL confirm that you're the person in control of that domain. Only the domain owner can add one.

Step by step

1

Copy your REAL verification code

Go back to your dashboard and copy the code from your Website verification card. It looks like REAL-XXXXXXXX. You'll need it in step 3.

2

Log into where you bought your domain

This is usually Namecheap, GoDaddy, Squarespace, or wherever you registered your domain. Select your registrar below for exact instructions.

Log in → click Domain List → click Manage next to your domain → click the Advanced DNS tab → scroll to the Host Records section.
Log in → click your name top right → My Products → find your domain and click DNS → scroll to the Records section.
Log in → go to Domains → click your domain → click DNS Settings → scroll to Custom Records.
Log in and look for a section called DNS Settings, DNS Management, Advanced DNS, or Manage DNS. Every registrar has one — it's usually under your domain's settings.
3

Add a new TXT record

Look for an Add Record button. Select TXT as the type, then fill in the fields exactly like this:

Type TXT
Host @
Value REAL-PQ5UTE (your code from the dashboard)
TTL Automatic (or leave as default)

The Host field might be labelled Name on some registrars — they mean the same thing. The @ symbol means "this domain".

4

Save and go back to your dashboard

Click Save (or Confirm, or Add Record — whatever the button says). Then go back to your REAL dashboard and click Check my DNS on your Website card.

⏱

It can take up to 30 minutes to update. If Check my DNS doesn't work straight away, wait a few minutes and try again. This is completely normal — it's just the internet catching up.
